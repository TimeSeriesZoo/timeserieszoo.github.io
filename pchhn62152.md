# PCHN62152: Advanced Image Analysis

For students in the Advanced Image Analysis class, please use this page as a guide
for the asynchronous materials for the session on 'Periodic and Aperiodic Signals'.

## Learning Objectives

- To understand how basic principles of digital signal processing relate to the study of neuro-electrophysiological recordings such as M/EEG
- To learn to identify potential pitfalls in the extraction and interpretation of featurs of interest in M/EEG data
- To understand and be apply the spectral parameterization methods for extraction periodic and aperiodic features of interest from neural power spectra

## Tutorial

The tutorial materials are organized into 4 sections:

- [01 - Overview & Introduction](https://timeserieszoo.github.io/tutorial/01_overview)
- [02 - Signals & Transforms](https://timeserieszoo.github.io/tutorial/02_sigs)
- [03 - Neuro-electrophysiological signals](https://timeserieszoo.github.io/tutorial/03_ephys)
- [04 - Spectral parameterization](https://timeserieszoo.github.io/tutorial/04_specparam)

Each of these materials is directly accessible through the table of contents on the left of the page.

On the table of contents you will also see additional links to other pages that include additional information and visualizers on related topics. You do not need to go to these - thought they may be useful and informative to explore.

## Further Reading

The following projects & tools were used to create the materials and visualizations used in the tutorial, and also include further reading, tutorials, and examples:

- [Oscillation methods project](https://oscillationmethods.github.io/)
- The [neurodsp](https://neurodsp-tools.github.io/neurodsp/) Python for digital signal processing
- The [specparam](https://specparam-tools.github.io/) Python module for spectral parameterization
